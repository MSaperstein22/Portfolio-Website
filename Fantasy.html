Overview

As someone who grew up playing fantasy football each year, I became frustrated with the inaccuracy of preseason fantasy football projections. This inspired me to create ValueBall, a data-driven approach to better evaluate wide receivers. Traditional analyst rankings often relied on recency bias, failing to factor in predictive metrics like targets. I developed a scoring system that assigns a point value to targets alongside traditional stats like yards and touchdowns, creating a more accurate player valuation model. The system, first implemented in a spreadsheet and later expanded into a Python script, identifies undervalued and overvalued players, giving fantasy managers a strategic edge.

My Impact

ValueBall has helped me make smarter roster decisions by highlighting players poised for future success based on their target volume. By automating data collection and analysis with web scraping and visualization tools, I transformed a manual spreadsheet into a dynamic, data-driven system. My model successfully identified key buy-low and sell-high players, leading to better draft picks and trades in multiple seasons of fantasy football. This project not only improved my fantasy football results but also deepened my understanding of data analytics and machine learning applications in sports.
