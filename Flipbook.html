Overview

As part of my Computer Science Education class, I had the opportunity to create an activity for fifth grade students about a powerful idea about computing. With three team members, I chose to design an activity to teach students about systems thinking and iteration through animation. As part of this activity I helped design and build a digital flipbook tool for students to manipulate parameters such as frames per second, rotation speed, and sprite size and see what happens in the animation. The session encouraged creativity and discovery as students engaged with animation concepts in a structured yet open-ended way.

My Impact

Since this activity was designed for fifth graders, I thought it would be very important to give them creative options for customizing the animation shown on screen. I designed the ability for the user to choose their own background and character from 4 different options for each part of the animation. I also implemented the play button, the pause button, and the frames per second slider so that students can explore what happens to the animation as they interact with these three features. I also helped make the design of the web application very clean and concise so it would be easy for young students to use without being given detailed instructions.
