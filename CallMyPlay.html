CallMyPlay: A Football Play Call Model

A model for NFL coaches that relies on historical game data to predict whether to run, pass, punt, or kick a field goal in a given game situation.

Overview

For my Sports, Technology, and Learning class, I worked with two friends to create CallMyPlay, a football play-calling model designed to help coaches make data-driven decisions rather than relying on emotion during high-pressure moments. By analyzing over 10 years of NFL play-by-play data, the model evaluates play outcomes using Expected Points Added (EPA) and recommends the optimal play type—run, pass, punt, or field goal—based on situational inputs. The model categorizes plays into key scenario "buckets" to ensure more accurate comparisons and decision-making. With a simple command-line interface, coaches can quickly input game situations and receive recommendations backed by historical analytics.

My Impact

I worked on creating the different scenario “buckets” based on which play situations are similar. I implemented this using down, distance, field position, time remaining, and score to place play scenarios into categories. I then implemented these buckets into the model’s algorithm to ensure that each play type recommendation is only based on similar past scenarios. I also helped test the model with various possible play types to verify that the model’s recommendations are reasonable and advantageous.
